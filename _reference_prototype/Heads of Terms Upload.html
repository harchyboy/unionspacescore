Post-Signature Modifications

Later additions become direct UNION agreements

Scope Lock Principle

Service scope is fixed at signing. Any additions or modifications requested after signature execution become separate direct UNION agreements, ensuring clarity and preventing scope creep on the original contract.

Modification Workflow

1
Tenant Requests Addition

New service request submitted via ticket system or direct contact with account manager.

2
Scope Assessment

UNION evaluates if addition fits within existing agreement or requires new direct contract.

3
Generate Direct Agreement

New standalone UNION agreement created with specific terms for the additional service.

4
Update Live Table

All-Inclusive table automatically reflects new addition with updated monthly totals.

Service Deployed

New service goes live with full documentation and audit trail maintained.

Example Scenarios

Adding Gym Membership Service
Tenant requests access to building gym facilities 3 months post-move. New direct UNION agreement created for £45/person/month. Live table updates to show new monthly cost.
Upgrading Internet Bandwidth
Original 1Gbps line insufficient. Tenant requests upgrade to 10Gbps. New agreement for incremental cost (£1,200/month). Original agreement remains unchanged; new service tracked separately.
Additional Meeting Room AV
Tenant expands to adjacent space, needs AV in 2 new rooms. Direct agreement for £750/month. Existing 4-room AV service continues under original agreement.

Landlord Appetite & Service Variability

Understanding what stays bundled vs moves to direct contracts

Commercial Dynamics

Landlord appetite for bundled services varies by property and relationship. High-consumption services (coffee beans) or specialty items (AV customization) often move to direct UNION contracts to maintain flexibility and prevent disputes.

High Landlord Appetite

Core infrastructure (internet, security, access)

Standard cleaning and waste management

Predictable, fixed-scope services

Services enhancing property value

Low Landlord Appetite

High-consumption items (coffee beans, supplies)

Specialty/bespoke services (custom AV)

Variable-usage services with unpredictable costs

Tenant-specific customizations

Decision Framework

Assess Landlord Relationship

Strong relationship = more bundling opportunities. New/transactional relationship = keep scope conservative.

Evaluate Cost Predictability

Fixed costs = bundle-friendly. Variable/usage-based = direct contract safer.

Consider Service Complexity

Standard services = bundle. Specialty/bespoke = direct to maintain flexibility.

Document Decision Rationale

Record why each service is bundled or direct for future reference and consistency.

Deal Type Selection Guidance

Choosing between All-Inclusive and Bolt-On structures

All-Inclusive

Current Deal

Services bundled via landlord. UNION has separate service agreement with landlord. Scope fixed at signing.

Characteristics

Landlord appetite for bundling

Simpler tenant experience (one bill)

UNION manages landlord relationship

Later additions = direct agreements

Best For

Landlords seeking premium tenants

Properties with strong amenities

Tenants wanting turnkey solutions

Bolt-On

Tenant has traditional lease. All UNION services are direct with UNION. Greater flexibility for customization.

Characteristics

Traditional landlord-tenant lease

Direct UNION-tenant relationship

Easier to modify service scope

More customization opportunities

Best For

Landlords preferring arms-length

Tenants with specific requirements

Existing leases adding UNION later

Preventing Unintentional Deal Type Changes

UI must guide choices carefully. Adding services post-signature should NOT flip the deal type. All-Inclusive deals remain All-Inclusive; additions become direct agreements. Bolt-On deals remain direct throughout. Clear labeling and confirmation prompts prevent confusion.
