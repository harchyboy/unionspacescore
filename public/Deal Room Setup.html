Future Service Additions

How new services are handled post-signature

Critical Policy

Regardless of whether this deal is All-Inclusive or Bolt-On, any service additions requested after the UNION agreement is signed will always be handled as direct UNION agreements with the tenant.

Why This Matters

Prevents scope creep in bundled services. Maintains clear boundaries. Allows flexibility without renegotiating primary agreements.

Tenant Experience

Simple add-on process through UNION portal. Clear pricing. No landlord negotiation required. Immediate service activation.

New Service Request

Tenant identifies need for additional service (e.g., gym access, bike storage, event catering)

Direct Agreement

UNION creates separate agreement with tenant. No landlord involvement. Clean contractual separation.

Fast Activation

Service deployed quickly without renegotiating bundle scope or primary lease terms.

Landlord Appetite & Positioning

Context for this deal structure

Landlord Preference: All-Inclusive

Moorgate Properties Group prefers bundled service packages as part of their premium building offering. They view comprehensive service delivery as a competitive advantage and are willing to carry the contractual obligation.

UNION's Role

UNION maintains a separate service agreement with the landlord to deliver bundled services. We manage all supplier relationships, quality standards, and day-to-day operations. The landlord relationship enables scale and efficiency.

Tenant Experience

From the tenant's perspective, UNION is the single point of contact for all services—whether bundled through the landlord or direct agreements. This creates a seamless experience while maintaining proper contractual clarity behind the scenes.

Flexibility vs. Predictability

The hybrid approach (some bundled, some direct) balances landlord desire for predictable scope with tenant need for flexibility on variable/specialty services. This structure has proven optimal for similar deals in the portfolio.
